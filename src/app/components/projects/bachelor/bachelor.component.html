Bachelor Thesis:
Controlling virtual agents in an urban traffic simulation

For my bachelor thesis at FSU Jena, I used reinforcement learning (specifically, Proximal Policy Optimization) to teach virutal cars how to navigate a simulated road network. My approach is built on Unity's ML-Agents toolkit and a proprietary road simulation library developed at my University.
I designed a training environment including several crossings and curves in addition to multiple configurations that were used to run three different experiments.
I received a grade of 1.0.
